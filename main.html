    Cricket, a bat-and-ball game, originated in England, likely in the Saxon or Norman periods (11th-13th centuries). It became a popular sport in the 18th century and spread with the British Empire. The Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) is the guardian of the Laws of Cricket.

    • Background

      Cricket is a team sport played with a bat and ball, where two teams of eleven players compete. The objective is to score more runs than the opposing team.

    • Foundation

      A cricket foundation can refer to various entities involved in the sport, including organizations focused on training, development, philanthropy, or even specific cricket stadiums.

    • Expansions and termination

      The main objective of each team is to score more runs than their opponents, but in some forms of cricket, it is also necessary to dismiss all but one of the opposition batters in their final innings in order to win the match.

    3.

    Cricket administration encompasses the bodies that govern and organize the sport at various levels, from international to local. The key players include the International Cricket Council (ICC), which is the global governing body, and national boards like the BCCI (Board of Control for Cricket in India).

  3. Organisation
    4.

    The primary global governing body for cricket is the International Cricket Council (ICC).The ICC is responsible for organizing major international tournaments like the Cricket World Cup, T20 World Cup, and the World Test Championship

    Organisation
    • Tournament format

      Organizing a successful cricket tournament requires careful planning and execution. Key steps include forming a committee, setting dates and a venue, deciding on the tournament format, creating a budget, and promoting the event.

    • Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries

      In cricket, player acquisition, squad composition, and salaries are crucial aspects of team management, especially in leagues like the IPL. Teams acquire players through auctions, trading, and replacement signings.

    • Match rules

      The batting team aims to score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets, while the fielding team tries to dismiss the batsmen and restrict their scoring.

    • Prize money

      The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is delighted to announce a cash reward of INR 58 Crore for Team India following their triumph at the ICC Champions Trophy 2025.
